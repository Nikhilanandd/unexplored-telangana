# Unexplored Warangal

Warangal, the capital of the Kakatiya dynasty, is a city where history is carved into stone. Beyond the well-known landmarks, its landscape is dotted with ancient temples, massive rock formations, and remnants of a glorious past that often go unnoticed by the casual traveler.

---

## 1. Thousand Pillar Temple (Rudreshwara Swamy Temple)
- **Location:** Hanamkonda.
- **Historical Background:** Built by King Rudra Deva in 1163 AD, it is a masterpiece of Kakatiya architecture dedicated to Shiva, Vishnu, and Surya.
- **Architectural Features:** Features a star-shaped plan, elaborate perforated stone screens, and a massive monolithic Nandi carved from a single basalt rock.
- **Cultural Importance:** A testament to the advanced engineering and artistic prowess of the Kakatiya era.

## 2. Warangal Fort
- **Location:** Warangal.
- **Historical Background:** Originally built by King Ganapati Deva in the 13th century and completed by his daughter, Rani Rudrama Devi.
- **Architectural Features:** Famous for its four massive stone gateways (Swayambhu Keerthi Thoranas) and the ruins of the central Swayambhu temple.
- **Significance:** Represents the defensive might of the Kakatiyas with its three layers of fortification walls.

## 3. Kush Mahal
- **Location:** Inside Warangal Fort.
- **Historical Background:** Built in the 14th century, likely by the Tughlaq dynasty or local governors after the fall of the Kakatiyas.
- **Architectural Features:** An audience hall with a sloping wall design characteristic of Tughlaq architecture. It now serves as a museum for Kakatiya artifacts.
- **Why it's unexplored:** Often overshadowed by the nearby Keerthi Thoranas, it provides a unique contrast of Indo-Persian style within a Hindu fort.

## 4. Padmakshi Temple
- **Location:** Hanamkonda.
- **Historical Background:** A 12th-century temple dedicated to Goddess Padmakshi, built on a hilltop.
- **Architectural Features:** Features a unique "Annapurna" style shikhara and ancient Jain carvings on the surrounding rocks, indicating it was once a Jain center.
- **Geographical Features:** Built into a giant granite rock, offering a panoramic view of Hanamkonda.

## 5. Bhadrakali Temple
- **Location:** Near Hanamkonda.
- **Historical Background:** One of the oldest temples for Goddess Bhadrakali in India, dating back to 625 AD (Chalukya period), later patronized by Kakatiyas.
- **Architectural Features:** The deity is carved from a single stone, and the temple sits on the banks of the scenic Bhadrakali Lake.
- **Significance:** A major pilgrimage site that combines natural beauty with ancient spirituality.

## 6. Govindarajula Gutta
- **Location:** Near Warangal Railway Station.
- **Historical Background:** A hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Govindaraja Swamy.
- **Architectural Features:** A long flight of steps leads to the top, where a small temple and a chariot are located.
- **Why it's unexplored:** It offers one of the best sunset views in the city but remains largely a local secret compared to the main temples.

## 7. Siddeshwara Temple
- **Location:** Hanamkonda.
- **Historical Background:** A small but ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, predating the Thousand Pillar Temple.
- **Architectural Features:** Built in the typical Western Chalukyan style with intricate carvings on the pillars and doorway.
- **Why visit:** For its peaceful atmosphere and fine details that reflect early Kakatiya artistic evolution.

## 8. Ramappa Temple (Rudreshwara Temple)
- **Location:** Palampet (approx. 70km from Warangal).
- **Historical Background:** Built in 1213 AD by Recharla Rudra, a general of Ganapati Deva. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
- **Architectural Features:** Known for its "floating bricks," sand-box foundation, and exquisite black basalt carvings of flute players and dancers.
- **Significance:** Described as the "brightest star in the galaxy of temples" by Marco Polo.

## 9. Laknavaram Lake
- **Location:** Govindaraopet (approx. 75km from Warangal).
- **Historical Background:** A massive reservoir constructed by the Kakatiyas by damming small streams.
- **Features:** It has about 13 tiny islands and a famous 160-meter long suspension bridge connecting three of them.
- **Why visit:** For its stunning natural beauty, boating, and the unique experience of walking across the suspension bridge.

## 10. Kazipet Dargah (Hazarat Syed Shah Afzal Biabani)
- **Location:** Kazipet.
- **Historical Background:** The tomb of a 19th-century Sufi saint who preached communal harmony.
- **Cultural Importance:** A symbol of the syncretic culture of the region, visited by people of all faiths.
- **Architectural Features:** A serene structure with a traditional dome and white-washed walls, surrounded by old trees.
