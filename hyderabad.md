# Unexplored Hyderabad

While Hyderabad is famous for the Charminar and Golconda Fort, many of its most significant historical and architectural treasures remain outside the popular tourist circuit.

---

## 1. Paigah Tombs
- **Location:** Pisal Banda, Kanchanbagh.
- **Historical Background:** These are the necropolis of the noble Paigah family, who were fierce loyalists to the Nizams. Built in the late 18th century.
- **Architectural Features:** Unique mix of Indo-Islamic, Mughal, and Greek styles. Known for intricate lime-plaster (stucco) work and exquisite marble screens (jalis).
- **Why it's unexplored:** Tucked away in narrow lanes, it lacks the commercial visibility of other monuments.

## 2. Badshahi Ashurkhana
- **Location:** Near High Court, Pathargatti.
- **Historical Background:** Built by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1594 to house the Alams (battle standards) during Muharram.
- **Architectural Features:** Famous for its stunning Persian-style enamel tiled mosaics (mosaic enamel work) which have retained their color for over 400 years.
- **Cultural Importance:** One of the oldest structures in the city, representing the Shia heritage of the Qutb Shahi dynasty.

## 3. Spanish Mosque (Aiwan-e-Begumpet)
- **Location:** Begumpet.
- **Historical Background:** Commissioned by Sir Vicar-ul-Umra, a Paigah noble, after his travels to Spain.
- **Architectural Features:** Built in the Moorish (Hispanic-Islamic) style, reminiscent of the Cathedral-Mosque of Córdoba. It features pointed arches and a unique exterior without traditional minarets.

## 4. Raymond’s Tomb
- **Location:** Asmangadh, Saroomagar.
- **Historical Background:** The burial site of Michel Joachim Marie Raymond, a French general in the service of the second Nizam.
- **Cultural Importance:** Locally known as "Moosa Ram," he was highly respected by both Hindus and Muslims, a rare example of a European becoming part of local folklore.
- **Geographical Features:** Located on a hillock with a panoramic view of the old city.

## 5. Bansilalpet Stepwell
- **Location:** Secunderabad.
- **Historical Background:** A multi-layered heritage stepwell that was neglected and used as a garbage dump for decades before its recent restoration.
- **Architectural Features:** A massive, tiered structure with intricate carvings, showcasing ancient rainwater harvesting engineering.

## 6. Hayat Bakshi Mosque
- **Location:** Hayathnagar.
- **Historical Background:** Built in 1672 by Hayat Bakshi Begum, the daughter of one King, wife of another, and mother of a third.
- **Architectural Features:** A grand Qutb Shahi style mosque with 5 arched openings and two massive minarets. It includes a massive caravan-sarai (rest house) for travelers.

## 7. Saidanima Tomb
- **Location:** Near Tank Bund (Upper Kawadiguda).
- **Historical Background:** Built by Sardar Abdul Haq, a high-ranking official in the Nizam’s Railways, for its mother Saidanima in the 1880s.
- **Architectural Features:** A fusion of Mughal and Qutb Shahi styles with a large bulbous dome and intricate stucco work.

## 8. Moula Ali Hill (Dargah)
- **Location:** Moula Ali.
- **Historical Background:** A 16th-century Dargah dedicated to Hazrat Ali, built on a giant monolithic rock.
- **Geographical Features:** 484 steps lead to the top, offering one of the best 360-degree views of the Hyderabad metropolitan area.

## 9. Mushirabad Mosque (Old Mosque)
- **Location:** Musheerabad.
- **Historical Background:** A grand Qutb Shahi era mosque, often overlooked because it is partially obscured by modern constructions and a busy market.
- **Architectural Features:** Similar in design to the Charminar and Mecca Masjid, featuring traditional Qutb Shahi arches and medallions.

## 10. Mons. Raymond's Obelisk (Asmangadh)
- **Location:** Malakpet/Asmangadh.
- **Historical Background:** Adjacent to Raymond's Tomb, this black granite obelisk stands 7 meters high.
- **Architectural Features:** Minimalist French memorial style, inscribed with the initials "J.R." (Joachim Raymond).
